Past Research/Projects

Ultraviolet Transitions in Neutral Chlorine

Joined the AMO Research group at UToledo my sophomore year. My role in this project was learning general atomic physics, learning the operation of the accelerator, and collecting data.

Ultraviolet Transitions in Sulfur II

In this project we were looking at finishing data acquisition and analysis on S II transitions. Here, I learned how to personally select materials to work with, build an ion source, and operate the accelerator. I also learned basic data analysis. The goal of this project was to verify whether or not cascade repopulation is affecting the primary transitions of S II at the transitions of interest.

The effects of Lossy compression on ATLAS data

In summer 2020, I did a virtual internship with ANL. I was assigned a project in collaboration with CERN.

The goal of this project was to test whether Lossy compression has a negative effect on ATLAS data. During this internship I learned how to interface with large servers, basic concepts of High Energy Physics, the PyROOT interface, and data analysis for large data sets.

Simulation of Atomic Physics with PyBeamng2

As the capstone project of my work in the THIA lab I decided to convert and update a legacy piece of software for simulating atomic physics using current models and Python. This project is currently in progress. In this project, I learned more complex atomic theory, as well as more on the complexity of THIA's beam conditions.
